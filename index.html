Unveiling the Legacy of Chinese Porcelain in Europe

This thesis examines the digitalization and depiction of Chinese porcelain collectionsin Europeana, a digital library of cultural heritage that includes metadata from culturalinstitutions across Europe. However, such artifacts are not without problemssuch as poor access, poor documentation, and a lack of standardized metadata standardsacross the contributing institutions. To this end, this study proposes a metadataschema based on an ontology that is specially designed for documenting and interpretingChinese porcelain artifacts in the Europeana environment. The “Chinese Porcelainin Europe” ontology created with Prot´eg´e included classes, properties, and relationshipsthat represent the richness of these artifacts in the Europeana context. Theontology is assessed and expanded by introducing instances derived from a set ofporcelain artifacts collected from the Europeana database. When examining the metadataof Europeana, trends in the distribution and digitization of Chinese porcelain inEuropean collections can be identified. The results also show the focus on items fromAustria, Belgium, and the Netherlands, which can be attributed to historical patterns ofcultural interchange. The assessment also shows that more attention should be paid tothe issue of metadata and its harmonization between the data providers representedin Europeana. A case study on the portrayal of Chinese female subjects on Europeanmadeporcelain endeavors to analyze such aspects as cultural appropriation and hybridaesthetics in the Europeana collection. The findings thus point to a selective assimilationof Chinese visual features, which could be interpreted as a reflection of Orientalistprinciples. In order to improve the accessibility and interest in this research, a websiteis created to display the ontology, metadata visualizations, and research findings. Itis also aimed at complementing the Europeana platform, ensuring the user-centeredapproach and compliance with the principles of digital humanities in the field of digitalcultural heritage. This work enriches the field of digital humanities by providing aframework for the management of metadata associated with Chinese porcelain on theEuropeana platform. It offers an understanding of the cultural processes behind thesecross-cultural products and serves as a useful tool for dissemination and exploration.Potential future works include the extension of the ontology, the use of enhanced visualizationmethods, language translation, and the use of linked data to enhance theappreciation of this valuable cultural asset within Europeana.

Covered Jar with Women on a Terrace and Shishi Finial

Chinese Ladies Porcelain in Europe

This artifact falls under the category of Chinese ladies porcelain, featuring depictions of women on its surface.

Descriptive Metadata

  • Colored Drawing: Underglaze blue with overglaze red, black, and gold
  • Decorations: Two scenes - women in gardens, one with a rabbit and a fan, another with musical instruments
  • Description: Drum-shaped covered jar with monster head handles
  • Glaze: Blue and white porcelain with additional colors
  • Function: Decorative and possibly storage
  • Shape: Drum-shaped with a lid and a shishi (lion-dog) finial
  • Production Place: China
