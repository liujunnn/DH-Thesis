Research Overview

Exploring Digital Representations of Chinese Porcelain in Europe

Research Focus

Our research addresses the challenges faced by cultural institutions in managing and presenting Chinese porcelain collections digitally. We focus on developing an ontology-based multilingual terminology for Chinese ceramics, with the following key objectives:

  • Create a comprehensive metadata model for Chinese porcelain in European collections
  • Analyze data providers and geographical distribution of collections in Europeana
  • Develop visualizations to enhance understanding and accessibility of cultural artifacts
  • Examine the representation of Chinese aesthetics in European-made Chinese-style porcelain
  • Design an interactive website to present research findings and improve public engagement

This research aims to contribute to the field of digital humanities by providing a framework for metadata management of Chinese porcelain on the Europeana platform, offering insights into cross-cultural artistic exchange, and enhancing the accessibility of these valuable cultural assets.

Research Methodology Timeline

1

Ontology Development

Using Protégé to create a structured representation of Chinese porcelain collections, including classes, properties, and relationships.

2

Metadata Analysis

Examining metadata from the Europeana collection to identify trends in distribution, digitization, and representation of Chinese porcelain.

3

Case Study Analysis

Conducting a focused study on the depiction of Chinese female figures on European-made porcelain, employing color analysis to explore themes of cultural appropriation and hybrid aesthetics.

4

Data Visualization

Utilizing Python, D3.js, and Chart.js to create interactive visualizations that enhance understanding of the collections and research findings.

5

Web Development

Designing and implementing an interactive website to present the ontology, metadata visualizations, and research outcomes, ensuring accessibility and user engagement.

Research Findings

Key Insights and Discoveries

Data Provider Analysis

Top contributors to the dataset:

  • Museum of Applied Arts (MAK)
  • Royal Institute for Cultural Heritage
  • Rijksmuseum

Geographical Distribution

Distribution of Chinese porcelain collections:

  • Austria: 22.4%
  • Belgium: 19.4%
  • Netherlands: 16.3%
  • Sweden: 10.7%

Temporal Distribution

68.4% of the entries originate from the period between 1700 and 1749, reflecting the peak of European fascination with Chinese porcelain.

This period marks the height of the "Chinoiserie" trend in European decorative arts

Case Study: Chinese Female Images

Color analysis of Chinese female figures on European-made porcelain:

  • Blue: 50.14%
  • Cyan: 19.49%
  • Yellow: 15.97%
  • Red: 11.74%
  • Green: 2.51%
  • Magenta: 0.16%

Interpretation of Findings

The predominance of blue and cyan tones in the representation of Chinese female figures suggests a European interpretation of Chinese aesthetics, potentially reflecting Orientalist perspectives and the process of cultural adaptation.

These findings indicate a selective appropriation of Chinese visual elements, with European artisans emphasizing certain colors (particularly blues) that aligned with their own aesthetic traditions and technical capabilities in porcelain production.

Conclusion and Future Work

Our research has made significant contributions to the field of digital cultural heritage through the creation of an ontology-based metadata framework, visualization of metadata, and design of an interactive website for Chinese porcelain in Europe.

Future research directions include:

  • Expanding the ontology structure
  • Incorporating advanced visualization techniques
  • Conducting user experience studies
  • Integrating multilingual support

By pursuing these avenues, we aim to further enhance the understanding, accessibility, and appreciation of these valuable cultural artifacts in the digital realm.
